This is not an exhaustive list, but a good starting place for a beginner to get a feel for what you can expect from your hardware and software storage options and how easily that can be done. There are different types of wallets, and generally they can be categorized into three types: Hardware wallet, Software wallet and Paper wallet. The Paper wallet is likley the most technical so we won 't give an option for that here. Hardware wallets, often called cold-storage or cold wallets are far and above the safest of the options, as they store your cryptocurrency private keys offline. Keep in mind, you are actually storing your cryptocurrency on the blockchain, but to access it you need the private-public key pairs that only you should know. Any wallet for cryptocurrency stores the private keys, not the currency itself.

So now you know how it works and what the most secure is, it 's important to note that software wallets, referred to also as hot wallets, are a great option and often require less hassle to use and link to DeFi apps for investing, exchanges or otherwise. Additionally, they are not limited in the scope of cryptocurrency you can store on them except by the limitations placed on trade in your region. How far will your cryptocurrency convert? A good investment strategy is to make a trade based on how well your fiat currency converts to crypto. Buy when your dollar can go the furthest. Fiat currency is based on a national currency, like the dollar or the Yen.

If you are ready to get started, you may understand at this point that you can invest in a number of ways. A more famous of the strategies (or infamous) is "Hodl-ing "from the acronym HODL (Hold On for Dear Life). This is commonly used in the crypto-community in reference to the general upward trend of cryptocurrency value despite a volatile market that fluctuates wildly on an hourly basis (reference the Ethereum price tracker above). This is likely second to the lowest-impact investing strategy for your money as it involves nothing but purchasing and eventually selling.

Another way to invest, is more of a crossover between Mining and the HODL strategy: Staking. If you haven 't taken a look above, Staking is where you put your money on a Proof of Work blockchain to secure the blockchain and thereby earn continually accruing parts of coins from that chain. For instance, you buy and hold Algorand and receive what is essentially interest. However, instead of strictly growing your dollar amount invested, which fluctuates with the market, you are growing the amount of cryptocurrency in that particular coin you hold. So, in our example, you are earning more Algorand steadily. This is often superior to just HODLing as you are also generating new coins for yourself which is like compounding your dollar gains.

Depending on the exchange or wallet application you use, you can stake different coins. On Coinbase exchange, for instance, you can stake Algorand, Tezos, Dai and Cosmos as well as USD Coin. You can 't stake from the Coinbase wallet , but you can from the Binance Wallet . There are a lot of options out there so dig around. Check out this list of some of the best:

Cryptocurrency Wallet List for 2021

A last strategy that we can discuss for beginners is utilizing Decentralized Finance (DeFi) applications. This may be slightly more advanced than the first two, but you can still totally do it. This strategy involves linking a wallet of any kind to an application and sending your money to that application to lend out for you so you can earn interest. Most DeFi applications are built on Ethereum, so you will need to have a store of that to send your money to the DeFi apps. Once you send you loan your money on those applications, via smart contracts, you get Tokens in exchange that can then be redeemed at any time for their value and interest earned in the like currency. For instance, you use visit Compound.Finance and link your Coinbase wallet with the application. Then you lend out a cryptocurrency of your choosing as supported by Compound and start earning Interest. Pretty simple. One thing to keep in mind is that you are spending Ethereum Gas fees to move your money around the blockchain at each step: Linking the wallet to lending to enabling earnings. That 's three different gas fees. Don 't know what the price of gas is on a particular day? Check out the Ethereum Block Explorer . Gas is essentially the fee that supports mining, and how miners get paid. Gas refers to the fee required to successfully conduct a transaction on Ethereum. In essence, gas fees are paid in Ethereum 's native currency, ether (ETH). Gas prices are denoted in Gwei, which itself is a denomination of ETH - each Gwei is equal to 0.000000001 ETH (10-9 ETH).You can take it a step further and Yield farm, but that has some significant risks. Yield farming is taking the tokens you receive from the DeFi apps you originally invested in and re-supplying them to another liquidity pool on places like Yearn or Curve . It works on the same principal, and because of the way the Ethereum blockchain is structured you earn the same ways but often at higher rates.

On a fundamental level, we are talking about mining cryptocurrency on a Proof of Work (PoW) blockchain or staking cryptocurrency on a Proof of Stake (PoS) blockchain. On these, you are validating transactions between users by dedicating your computer 's hashing power on PoW or currency on PoS blockchains respectively. You dedicate, on a sliding scale dependent on several factors, how intensive your resource devotion will be as you mine. However, miners are people who spend time, money and computational resources to drive the cryptocurrency networks for certain coins. The more resources you dedicate, as long as you are making smart choices we will discuss here, generally you will get a higher return. That return is derived from balancing electricity costs, one-time hardware costs and profits from mining as determined by the hashrate of your equipment. For instance, you can mine on a laptop passively, with a low hash-rate and spend very little time and money to get very small mining returns by cloud mining. Conversely, you could balance equipment costs, energy prices and market pricing to build a custom mining rig and get significantly higher rewards.
